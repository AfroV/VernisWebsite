2. Definitions

  • "Vernis NFT" — A signed, numbered Ethereum-based non-fungible token. Each edition is uniquely numbered and recorded on-chain.
  • "Preservation Kit" — A set of individually CE-marked components and a custom enclosure, shipped to the NFT holder upon claiming. The kit includes: Raspberry Pi 5, 4" IPS display, 128GB storage with pre-loaded software, PETG enclosure, power supply, and assembly guide.
  • "Claiming" / "Redemption" — The process of burning the Vernis NFT to receive the Preservation Kit.
  • "Unclaimed NFT" — A Vernis NFT that has not been burned/redeemed.

3. What You Are Purchasing

When you mint a Vernis NFT, you are purchasing:

  • A signed, numbered digital collectible (NFT) on Ethereum.
  • The right to claim one (1) Vernis Preservation Kit by burning the NFT.
  • The right to trade the unclaimed NFT on secondary markets.

The NFT is the primary product — a collectible digital artwork. The Preservation Kit is provided as a benefit to NFT holders. The NFT does not constitute ownership of any intellectual property, company shares, or future revenue.

3a. Nature of the Preservation Kit

The Preservation Kit is a component kit, not an assembled electronic product. It contains individually CE-marked components (Raspberry Pi 5, display, power supply) alongside a passive PETG enclosure and assembly instructions. The buyer assembles the kit themselves.

Each electronic component carries its original manufacturer's CE marking and warranty. Vernis does not manufacture, modify, or assemble these electronic components. The pre-loaded software on the SD card is open-source and provided "as is."
